## About

Medicare fraud costs American taxpayers billions of dollars annually. The Department of Justice, HHS Office of Inspector General, and FBI's Healthcare Fraud Unit coordinate to identify, investigate, and prosecute providers who defraud Medicare.

## Medicare Program Overview

Medicare is the federal health insurance program for:
- Americans 65 and older
- Younger people with certain disabilities
- People with End-Stage Renal Disease

Medicare Part A covers hospital care; Part B covers outpatient services; Part C (Medicare Advantage) are private plans; Part D covers prescription drugs. Total Medicare spending exceeds $800 billion annually.

## Scale of Medicare Fraud

CMS estimates that improper Medicare payments total tens of billions annually. The DOJ Health Care Fraud Unit and Medicare Fraud Strike Force have:
- Charged thousands of individuals in Medicare fraud schemes
- Recovered billions in fraud proceeds
- Excluded thousands of providers from Medicare

## Medicare Fraud Schemes by Category

### Home Health Fraud
False certifications that patients are homebound and need skilled nursing. Providers bill for visits that never occurred or for patients who don't qualify.

### DME Fraud
Durable medical equipment suppliers bill for wheelchairs, orthotics, back braces, and CPAP equipment that patients didn't need or didn't receive.

### Lab Testing Fraud
Laboratories submit massive numbers of unreasonable and unnecessary tests, often targeting Medicare patients through illegal telehealth prescriptions.

### Ambulance Fraud
Billing for emergency ambulance transport when patients could have used less expensive transportation.

### Hospice Fraud
Enrolling patients who don't meet terminal illness criteria for hospice, or billing for care not provided.

### Mental Health and Substance Abuse
Group therapy sessions that never occurred, individual therapy billed at group rates, sessions not provided by licensed therapists.

## Penalties for Medicare Fraud

- Up to 10 years per count under 18 U.S.C. § 1347
- Treble damages under False Claims Act
- Exclusion from all federal health programs
- Loss of professional licenses
- Civil monetary penalties
